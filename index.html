About the Event

A landmark moment for HNDIT Galle.

INNOVISION 2026 is the Department of Information Technology's annual Student Project Showcase & Innovation Exhibition — a professionally evaluated platform where undergraduate students present their best work to peers, academic staff, and industry professionals.

Under the theme "Vision Beyond Innovation", this year's exhibition celebrates technical excellence across five competition categories — including the newly introduced AI-Based Website category, reflecting the department's forward-looking commitment to emerging technologies.

Hosted at Advanced Technology Institute, Galle, and affiliated with SLIATE, INNOVISION 2026 is designed to be a repeatable annual standard and a permanent record of student innovation.

Competition

Five categories. One exhibition.

Each project is registered under exactly one category and evaluated by a dedicated panel of two to three judges.

Category 01

Web-Based System

Full-stack web applications with backend logic, database integration, and authentication. Includes management systems, portals, and e-commerce platforms.

100 marks

Category 02

Mobile Application

Native or cross-platform mobile applications for Android or iOS. Evaluated on mobile UX, functionality, and performance across real devices.

100 marks

Category 03

Desktop Application

Standalone software for Windows, macOS, or Linux. Judged on GUI design, stability, architecture, and distribution readiness.

100 marks

Category 04

Arduino Project

Hardware-software integrated projects using Arduino or compatible microcontrollers. Open to individual or group submissions — judged on circuit design, functionality, and code quality.

100 marks

Category 05

AI-Based Website

Websites integrating Artificial Intelligence — chatbots, recommendation engines, NLP, image recognition, or predictive analytics. Evaluated on AI quality, design, and real-world utility.

100 marks

Evaluation

Judging process

Step 01

Booth assignment & registration

Each team is allocated a designated exhibition booth or demonstration station on event day.

Step 02

10-minute presentation window

Participants demonstrate their project and answer judge questions within a strict 10-minute slot.

Step 03

Private mark sheet submission

Judges score independently using the official 100-mark rubric and submit sealed sheets to the Chief Coordinator.

Step 04

Score aggregation & verification

The Event Committee tallies and verifies all scores before any placements are announced.

Step 05

Tie-break & awards ceremony

In the event of a tied placement, judges convene for a final collective decision. Medals and certificates are awarded.

Panel composition

Every category is evaluated by two to three judges — at least one senior academic staff member from the Department of IT, and where possible an industry professional or alumni guest to bring real-world evaluation standards. Judges have no prior access to projects they evaluate.

Certificates

Every participant receives recognition.
